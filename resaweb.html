Description

ResaWeb became a significant educational project during the second semester of my studies at IUT. The assignment required creating a website for reserving products or services. This website involved working with data models and establishing a database. The project didn't mandate the creation of user accounts and a shopping cart, but it was necessary to organize a system capable of generating product pages automatically based on query data. Additionally, it needed to handle client orders by storing them in the database and sending email notifications.

Regarding design, the site had to display correctly between 768 and 1280 pixels and ideally have a mobile version. It also had to comply with WCAG AA standards and pass accessibility checks according to Opquast rules.

For the project idea, I chose a service that offers photographers the organization of tourist trips, including not only sightseeing but also photography workshops. The service also provides equipment rental during journeys, allowing photographers to try out new cameras, lenses, and more.

As the project idea is very dear to me, creating a visually appealing design that is simultaneously aesthetic and user-friendly was crucial. For the first time, I was able to work with the MidJourney AI, which helped me generate images for all proposed tours with minor correction in Photoshop after.

The project also complies with accessibility requirements, such as skip links and alternative texts.

The site features a search bar (PHP), filters (JavaScript), and sorting by price (PHP). The order page includes JavaScript to calculate the final cost, considering various conditions like travel in the order, the quantity of gadgets for rent, the number of days, etc.
